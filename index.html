Current Research

I currently work as a Graduate Research Assistant in the Mechanisms of Individualized Neurorehabilitation Treatment Laboratory (MINT Lab) at Georgia State University. My work focuses on reading, language, and neurorehabilitation in adults with aphasia.

  • Reading performance and attitudes in adults with aphasia.
  • Contributions to neuromodulation and reading studies.
  • Assessment administration (e.g., WAB, TOWRE, CARA, SAPA).
  • Data organization, scoring, and basic analysis support.
  • Participant interaction and structured research tasks.

Additional details about my research and clinical experience are available in my CV.

Selected Projects

Reading in Aphasia Project

Mixed-methods study examining relationships between standardized reading scores and self-reported reading attitudes in people with aphasia using tools such as the WAB, CARA, and TOWRE-2.
